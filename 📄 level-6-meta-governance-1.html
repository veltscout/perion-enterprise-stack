Framework Abstract

This document establishes a comprehensive governance hierarchy designed to protect the accuracy and consistency of Perion Lighting's technical and commercial records. By implementing a six-level control framework, the organization ensures that engineering specifications remain the "technical truth" and are never diluted by marketing or regional adaptations. The system introduces a mandatory "Tender Freeze" protocol, which locks verified documentation to provide a legally defensible and unalterable foundation during active bidding cycles. Ultimately, this framework centralizes accountability and version control, mandating that only specific authorized leaders can approve changes to the company's definitive master archive.

The transition from a basic governance structure to a robust enterprise documentation control framework is a mandatory evolution for organizations operating within complex engineering, procurement, and high-security environments. This framework, established as Level 6 (Meta / Governance), serves as the authoritative layer that governs the creation, validation, storage, and distribution of all technical and commercial artifacts within the system. By establishing a canonical layout authority and strict level separation logic, the framework ensures that "Technical Truth" is preserved across the entire documentation stack, preventing the phenomenon of "document drift" which frequently compromises the integrity of technical specifications during marketing distillation or multi-regional expansion. This report outlines the mandatory rules, structural hierarchies, and digital control environments required to maintain an audit-defensible and legally robust documentation ecosystem.

Section 1

Governance System Architecture

The governance system is architected as a six-level vertical hierarchy. Each level maintains a distinct functional boundary and audience, ensuring that technical engineering data is never conflated with commercial narratives or branding guidelines. This architecture is designed to facilitate a unidirectional flow of content validation while allowing for a structured escalation path for deviations or disputes.

1.1 Visual Hierarchy and Level Definitions

The hierarchical architecture ensures that every document is anchored to its functional purpose. The separation logic prevents content from "crossing levels," which is essential for maintaining the integrity of technical specifications during procurement cycles.

Level Functional Identity Governing Mandate Primary Audience
Level 1 Customer-Facing PDS Value proposition and market positioning. Customers, Distributors
Level 2 Technical TDS Single source of technical and engineering truth. Engineers, Technical Buyers
Level 3 Procurement & BOM Commercial execution and ordering logic. Procurement, Operations
Level 4 Compliance & Certs Evidence layer and regulatory declarations. Auditors, Tender Boards
Level 5 Brand & Design Visual system and layout control. Designers, Marketing
Level 6 Governance Authority Meta-control and structural authority. Executive Board, Auditors

1.2 Content Flow and Escalation Path

The content flow is governed by a technical-first logic. Engineering specifications defined at Level 2 provide the "Technical Truth" which informs the market-ready narratives at Level 1. Conversely, procurement logic at Level 3 must adhere to the constraints defined in Level 2. The escalation path for any deviation from this hierarchy is vertical. If a marketing requirement at Level 1 conflicts with an engineering specification at Level 2, the dispute must be escalated to the Level 6 Governance Authority for resolution. Unauthorized modifications to the canonical layout at any level are prohibited and trigger an immediate "Invalidation Notice" from the Level 6 board.

Content Flow — Technical-First Logic

The flow of content begins at the technical authority layer (Level 2). Any specification change at this level triggers a mandatory assessment of the downstream levels, particularly Level 3 (Procurement) and Level 4 (Compliance). This ensures that the physical hardware listed in the Bill of Materials (BOM) remains in absolute alignment with the engineering certifications and the customer-facing data sheets.

Section 2

RACI Matrix (Mandatory Decision Authority)

The management of an enterprise-grade documentation stack requires the formal assignment of decision authority. This is achieved through a RACI matrix (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed), which defines the roles of Product Management, Engineering, Marketing, Operations, and the Executive Board across the document lifecycle.

2.1 Formal RACI Matrix

The following matrix establishes the mandatory roles for documentation control. In this framework, "Accountability" is restricted to a single role per task to ensure clarity of ownership.

Task / Activity Product Management Engineering Marketing Operations Executive
Content Creation A C C I I
Technical Validation C A I C I
Compliance Approval I R I C A
Final Release Auth C C C A C
Archive Control I I I A I
Structural Revision C C C R A
R Responsible — The entity that performs the work to complete the task.
A Accountable — The entity with the final decision authority and "veto" power.
C Consulted — Subject matter experts whose input is required before the task is finalized.
I Informed — Stakeholders who are notified of the task's completion or the decision's outcome.

2.2 Clarification of Final Decision Authority

Final decision authority for all technical specifications rests with the Engineering lead, while final release authorization for "Tender-Grade" documents rests with Operations. In the event of an irreconcilable conflict between departments, the Executive layer (CEO) serves as the ultimate arbiter, specifically for documents that carry legal or regulatory liability, such as Compliance Appendices.

Section 3

Escalation & Dispute Protocol

Disputes regarding document content or structural deviations must be resolved through a formal protocol to prevent the degradation of technical standards. The Level 6 Meta-Governance Board serves as the review board for all such escalations.

3.1 Engineering vs. Marketing Conflict Resolution

A common point of friction exists between the engineering requirement for technical accuracy and the marketing desire for simplified messaging. The protocol mandates that the Technical Data Sheet (TDS) at Level 2 is the primary authority. Marketing narratives at Level 1 may simplify information but must never contradict the engineering thresholds defined in Level 2. Any attempt to soften technical constraints for marketing purposes constitutes a violation of governance and shall be rejected by the Engineering lead during the review phase.

3.2 Compliance Dispute Escalation

Disputes regarding compliance status (Level 4) often occur when product updates move faster than third-party laboratory certifications. In such cases, the document status must remain in "REVIEW" or "DRAFT" and cannot be promoted to "FINAL" for external distribution. If a sales lead requires the document for a tender, the escalation path requires the CEO to sign a formal "Manufacturer's Declaration" to bridge the evidence gap, provided the engineering data supports the claim.

3.3 Distributor Deviation and Unauthorized Circulation

Distributors are prohibited from modifying any Perion Lighting document. Any deviation discovered in a regional market triggers an immediate "Unauthorized Document Circulation Response". This involves:

  1. Immediate notification to the distributor to cease distribution of the non-approved version.
  2. The issuance of the current "Controlled Release" version to all affected parties.
  3. A formal audit of the distributor's regional documentation portal.
Section 4

Audit & Review Cadence

Enterprise governance is not a static state but a continuous process of verification. The framework mandates a scheduled audit cadence to ensure all documentation remains current, compliant, and accurate.

4.1 Scheduled Review Cycles

The following review cycles are mandatory for the maintenance of the "Audit-Grade" status of the documentation stack:

  1. Annual Full Documentation Review: A comprehensive audit of every document in the /Perion_Documentation master repository. This review verifies that the structural integrity of the six levels is maintained and that no "structural drift" has occurred.
  2. Quarterly Compliance Review: A targeted audit of Level 4 documents. This cycle ensures that ISO certifications, laboratory test reports (e.g., IP69K), and EMC safety declarations are valid and that renewal processes have been initiated for any certificates nearing expiry.

4.2 Trigger Events for Forced Revision

In addition to scheduled reviews, certain events mandate an immediate "Forced Revision" of the documentation:

  1. Technical/Engineering Changes: Any change to hardware, such as a shift in LED binning or a change in the cable's UV-resistance compound, requires a major version update to Level 2 and Level 3 documents.
  2. Regulatory Updates: Changes in international safety or environmental standards (e.g., RoHS/REACH updates) mandate a revision of Level 4 compliance declarations.
  3. Distributor Inquiries/Legal Challenges: If a technical specification is formally challenged in a legal or procurement setting, the document must undergo an immediate verification audit by the Engineering and Compliance teams.

4.3 Expiry Policy for Outdated Certifications

Certifications that have reached their expiration date are considered "invalidating artifacts." The governance rule mandates that any document relying on an expired certification must be moved to "REVIEW" status and watermarked as "EXPIRED – NOT FOR TENDER SUBMISSION". It is the responsibility of Operations to track expiry dates in the Master Index and trigger the renewal process at least 90 Days prior to expiry.

Section 5

Digital Control Environment

The digital environment where the "Single Source of Truth" is housed must be managed with the same level of rigor as the documents themselves. Control over access, storage, and archival is central to maintaining an audit-defensible system.

5.1 Master File Storage and Access Hierarchy

The canonical master files must be stored in a centralized, secured location, specifically the /Perion_Documentation root folder. The subfolder structure is strictly defined to mirror the document hierarchy:

/Perion_Documentation — Root: Single Source of Truth /PDS — Level 1: Customer-facing sheets /TDS — Level 2: Technical engineering sheets /Procurement — Level 3: Ordering and BOM guides /Compliance — Level 4: Certifications and declarations /Brand — Level 5: Design assets /Archive — Superseded and obsolete documents

Access control follows a Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) model. Only the "Accountable" role for a specific level has editing rights, while all other stakeholders are restricted to "Read-Only" access for "FINAL" versions.

5.2 Version Locking and Backup Policy

Once a document is approved as "FINAL," it must be version-locked to prevent accidental modification. Any further changes require the initiation of a new version (e.g., from v1.0 to v1.1 or v2.0).

Backup Policy
The entire documentation repository must be backed up daily to a secure, off-site location to ensure disaster recovery capability.
Archive Retention
All documentation, particularly technical specifications and compliance evidence, must be retained for a minimum of 7 Years. This retention period is mandatory to meet legal and audit requirements in the mining and security infrastructure sectors where long-term liability is a factor.
Section 6

Tender Freeze & Submission Protocol

In the context of government and large-scale enterprise procurement, the stability of technical claims is paramount. The "Tender Freeze" protocol prevents the loss of credibility that occurs when technical specifications change mid-submission.

6.1 The Freeze Rule and Revision Prohibition

A "Tender Freeze" is a state where the active version of a document is locked and cannot be updated, regardless of any internal revisions, until the tender adjudication period is over.

Freeze Rule Specification
Prohibition of Mid-Tender Revision Revisions to submitted documents are prohibited once the tender deadline has passed, unless formally requested by the tender board for clarification.
Version Stamping Every document submitted must be stamped with a unique submission-specific version identifier (e.g., PRN-TDS-V1.0-TENDER-2026-REF01) to ensure the adjudicators are reviewing the exact canonical version intended for that bid.

6.2 Formal Supersession Rules

When a new version of a document is released after a tender freeze is lifted, it must follow formal supersession rules. The new version must explicitly state which version it replaces. The superseded version is then moved to the /Archive folder to prevent its accidental use in future tenders.

Section 7

Document Lifecycle Flow

The document lifecycle is a structured progression from an internal idea to an archived piece of historical evidence. Each stage has defined responsibilities and mandatory gates.

7.1 Lifecycle Stages

Stage Responsibility Primary Action / Gate
Draft Product Management Initial content creation and technical drafting.
Review Engineering / Marketing Technical and brand validation sweep.
Approved Executive Sign-Off Legal and compliance final authorization.
Released Operations Version locking and master storage placement.
Active Sales / Tenders Distribution to customers and external partners.
Superseded Operations Removal from active folders and archive tagging.
Archived Operations Long-term 7-Year storage for legal defensibility.

7.2 Gate Responsibilities

Promotion from Review to Approved is the most critical gate in the lifecycle. It requires a "Change-Impact Sweep" to ensure that the data being approved does not conflict with other levels. For example, if a Level 2 TDS update increases the power consumption of a linear perimeter illumination unit, the Level 3 Ordering Guide must be checked to ensure the Power Pack SKUs are still compatible.

Section 9

Structural Enforcement and Layout Authority

The canonical layout is the "visual container" for technical truth. It is ordered exactly as technical buyers and auditors expect: Overview → Engineering → Procurement → Compliance.

9.1 Mandatory Section Order

To maintain consistency across multi-regional versions, the section order defined in the "Master PDS/TDS Layout Structure" is mandatory:

  1. System Overview: Market positioning and high-level value.
  2. Engineering Specifications: Electrical, environmental, and mechanical thresholds.
  3. Procurement Logic: SKUs, ordering tables, and BOM instructions.
  4. Compliance Annexures: ISO certificates, lab test reports, and signatures.

9.2 Rule of Level Separation

Content must never cross levels. For example, detailed laboratory test data (Level 4) must not be embedded in the Customer-Facing PDS (Level 1). Instead, Level 1 documents should reference the existence of Level 4 certificates, maintaining a clean and professional information hierarchy. This separation ensures that each audience receives the appropriate density of information while maintaining a clear path back to the "Evidence Layer" for those who require it.

Level Separation — Enforcement Rule

Any attempt to embed raw laboratory test data (Level 4) into a Customer-Facing PDS (Level 1) constitutes a governance violation and triggers an immediate "Invalidation Notice" from the Level 6 Meta-Governance Board. Level 1 documents must utilize reference-only logic — acknowledging the existence of certifications without exposing the detailed engineering thresholds that define the system's performance.

This framework is the governing authority for all Perion Lighting artifacts and is to be enforced without exception.
