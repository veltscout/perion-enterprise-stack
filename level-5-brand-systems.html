Document Preamble — Governed by Level 6 Protocols

The Perion Brand and Documentation Governance Manual establishes the authoritative framework for the structural, typographic, and visual execution of all Product Data Sheets (PDS), Technical Data Sheets (TDS), and auxiliary compliance documentation. Within the Linear Perimeter Illumination Infrastructure environment, documentation serves as a primary vehicle for engineering defensibility and regulatory compliance.

The documentation stack is categorized into six hierarchical levels, ranging from Level 1 (Customer-Facing Overview) to Level 6 (Meta-Governance and Authority). This manual serves as the Level 5 layer, providing the specific visual and structural controls required to maintain the integrity of the entire documentation ecosystem.

Section 1

Style System Overview

The Perion InDesign Style System is the mandatory structural foundation for all official documentation. It defines the typographic, structural, and visual parameters that must be applied to every PDS and TDS.

Adherence to this style system is a primary metric of document control; manual overrides, local formatting, and ad-hoc styling are categorized as governance failures. The system functions as a "Single Source of Truth," where the paragraph and character style maps prevent stylistic deviations.

Single Source of Truth — Governance Mandate

Manual overrides, local formatting, and ad-hoc styling are categorized as governance failures under the Level 5 framework. The style maps prevent stylistic deviations that would compromise the "Technical Truth" of the documentation stack. Any document exhibiting unauthorized formatting is subject to immediate Withdrawal from Circulation.

Section 4

PDS vs. TDS Style Variants

The Perion documentation system maintains a strict "Structural Separation" between its commercial and technical documents.

4.1. The PDS Variant: Executive Narrative

The PDS is optimized for decision-makers and procurement officers who require a high-level understanding of system intent and value.

4.2. The TDS Variant: Engineering Truth

The TDS is intended for consulting engineers and technical evaluators. It prioritizes information density and professional neutrality.

PDS — Product Data Sheet: Executive Narrative
  • Heading Hierarchy: Limited to a maximum of two levels (H1 and H2).
  • Typography: Larger H1 headers with a lighter weight.
  • Whitespace: Strategic use of whitespace is mandated to facilitate scanning.
  • Tables: Restricted to "Summary Tables" which utilize rounded numeric precision and simplified units for ease of comparison.
TDS — Technical Data Sheet: Engineering Truth
  • Heading Hierarchy: Supports the full hierarchy (H1 through H3+) as required to categorize complex electrical and photometric data.
  • Typography: H1 headers are smaller but have more visual weight (boldness) to denote a dense, technical atmosphere.
  • Density: Moderate to high paragraph density is expected; the document is a "Fact Sheet" rather than a brochure.
  • Tables: Must include "Full Specification Tables" with exact values, comprehensive units, and declared tolerances.

4.3. Comparison of Variant Rules

Attribute PDS (Product Data Sheet) TDS (Technical Data Sheet)
Primary Audience Executives / Procurement. Engineers / Auditors.
Maximum H-Levels 2 Levels. Full Hierarchy (3+).
Iconography Permitted for feature highlights. Strictly Prohibited.
Numeric Precision Rounded / Summary. Exact / Full Declaration.
Callout Boxes Allowed for value-prop highlights. Restricted to safety warnings only.
Cross-Level Performance Value Prohibition

No performance value may be introduced in a PDS that is not already present and verified in the TDS.

5.2. Application Governance

3-Color Rule

Perion documentation follows a strict "3-Color Rule": no single page may exceed three primary colors (Midnight Blue, Graphite, and Safety Amber). Safety Amber is explicitly forbidden for decorative use; its presence must always indicate a safety-critical warning, a technical highlight, or an engineering note. Decorative use of Safety Amber constitutes a governance failure and triggers immediate document withdrawal.

Section 6

Layout Grid System

The Perion Grid System provides the spatial discipline required for professional document control.

6.1. Margin Standards and Column Structure

All documentation must conform to a standardized A4 page layout.

  • Page Margins: A generous margin profile is mandated to ensure that critical document metadata (Document ID, pagination) in the footer remains clear of the content zone.
  • Column Grid: A standard multi-column structure is used to manage content density. In PDS variants, columns are wider to support a narrative flow, while TDS variants may utilize a denser grid to accommodate side-by-side technical comparisons.
  • Line Length: Prose line length is capped at maximum characters; lines exceeding this length are considered a legibility failure.

6.2. Header and Footer Zones

The header and footer are "Governed Zones" that must not be encroached upon by body content.

Zone A
Header Zone
Reserved exclusively for the Perion and Sunbird logos. No decorative icons, text, or page titles are permitted in the header.
Zone B
Hero / Tech Title
High-resolution photograph (PDS) or bold H1 section header (TDS). No technical annotations on PDS cover hero.
Zone C
Title / Data Table
PDS_TDS_Title in Midnight Blue (PDS) or full-width specification table with Inline_Technical values (TDS).
Zone D
Body / Schematic
Two-column Body_Text narrative (PDS) or vector-based wiring diagram with ISO-standard line weights and IP69K callout boxes (TDS).
Zone E
Footer Zone
Contains the "Document Authority Layer": mandatory Document Title, unique Document ID (e.g., PRN-CPL-L4-001), and "Page X of Y" pagination in Footer_Text style.

6.3. Table Width and Alignment Rules

Tables are the backbone of technical documentation and must follow rigid alignment rules to prevent misinterpretation.

  • Width: All tables must span the full width of the text column; "floating" or partial-width tables are prohibited.
  • Alignment: Content within cells must be left-aligned for text and key fields to maintain a consistent vertical scan line. Column headers typically use centered alignment.
  • Styling: Use the Table_Header style for headers (Midnight Blue background, white text) and Table_Cell for data. Row banding using Perion Light Grey is mandatory for tables exceeding five rows to aid in row tracking.
Section 7

Iconography Standard

Perion's iconography is treated as a functional tool for supporting document hierarchy rather than a decorative element.

7.1. Icon Style and Categories

  • Style: All icons must feature a consistent line weight and stroke style that aligns with Perion's industrial aesthetic. They must be monochrome or utilize Perion brand colors strategically.
  • Approved Categories: Icons are approved for use as "Visual Cues" to reinforce value propositions (e.g., a "Zero Trenching" shovel icon) or as "Status Indicators" (e.g., ✓ to indicate a completed compliance level).
  • Prohibited Sources: Ad-hoc, downloaded, or third-party icons from unverified sources are strictly prohibited; all icons must be sourced from the approved internal brand library.

7.2. Usage Rules: PDS vs. TDS

Allowed
PDS — Product Data Sheet

Icons may be used strategically on cover pages and feature grids to highlight key system benefits and use cases.

Strictly Prohibited
TDS — Technical Data Sheet

To ensure that Technical Data Sheets remain "visually neutral" and "audit-safe," the use of icons or decorative visual cues is explicitly forbidden. Engineering data must stand on its own without visual "softening".

Technical Callout & Caption Governance

For ratings like IP69K, diagrams must include callout boxes detailing the test parameters: pressure (10 000 kPa), temperature (+60 °C), nozzle distance, and duration. Every diagram must have a caption prefixed with "Caption:" followed by a concise engineering explanation of the visual. Captions must be positioned directly below the visual element.

Section 9

File Naming and Version Control Protocol

The Perion Version Control Protocol is a mandatory governance layer.

9.1. File Naming Structure

Every controlled document must follow this mandatory naming formula:

Mandatory File Naming Formula [Company]-[Level]-[Product]-[DocType]-[Number]-v[Version].pdf Example: PRN-L5-BDS-001-v1.0-FINAL.pdf

9.2. Version Numbering and Ownership

vX.0 — Major Version
Triggered by a technical specification change, a hardware update, a structural layout change, or a compliance status update.
v0.Y — Minor Version
Triggered by editorial corrections, formatting clarifications, or minor branding updates.
Ownership Field
Every document must have a defined "Document Ownership Field" in its internal metadata, listing the department responsible for its content (e.g., Product Management for PDS, Engineering for TDS).

9.3. Revision History and Control Status

A "Revision History Block" is mandatory for every authoritative document. It must record the version number, the date of issue, a brief description of the change, and the authorized signatory.

Section 10

Accessibility and Legibility Controls

As an infrastructure provider, Perion is committed to ensuring its documentation is accessible to all stakeholders, including those using assistive technologies.

10.1. Typography and Contrast

Control Specification Governance Rule
Minimum Body Font Size 10 pt Anything smaller is a rejection criterion for official submission.
Minimum Compact Font Size 8 pt Reserved for compact technical data only; must not be used for primary narrative.
Contrast Ratio WCAG 2.1 AA minimum (4.5:1) Midnight Blue on Chalk White and Graphite on Light Grey palettes are specifically chosen to exceed these requirements.
Line Spacing (Leading) 120 % minimum Mandatory for all narrative paragraphs to ensure line-tracking ease for technical readers.

10.2. PDF Export and Tagging Requirements

All official PDF exports must be "Tagged" to ensure a logical reading order for screen readers.

PDF Element Required Tag Governance Note
Heading Styles <H1>, <H2>, <H3> Heading styles must map to appropriate HTML-style tags to maintain logical reading hierarchy for screen readers.
Body Text <P> All narrative paragraphs must be tagged as paragraph elements.
Decorative Elements Artifact Repeated headers, footers, and border lines must be marked as "Artifacts" so they are ignored by assistive technology.
Figures & Diagrams Alt-Text (embedded) All figures and diagrams must have descriptive alt-text embedded within the PDF properties to explain their purpose to visually impaired users.

11.2. Review Frequency and Deviation Control

  • Review Frequency: All Level 1–4 documents are subject to a mandatory annual review to ensure they reflect the current "Plan of Record" for the system.
  • Deviation Control: Any deviation from the "Master PDS/TDS Layout Structure" requires a formal Engineering Change Order (ECO) equivalent and approval from the Level 6 Meta-Governance board.
  • Audit Compliance Checklist: Before a document is released for a major tender, it must pass a "Tender-Ready" audit, verifying ISO 9001:2015 cert numbers, IP69K test lab IDs, and LM-80/TM-21 LED lifetime data.

Section 13

Enforcement and Compliance Statement

Adherence to this Brand and Documentation Governance Manual is a mandatory requirement for all Perion personnel, distributors, and partners. The documentation system is a controlled infrastructure asset, and its integrity is essential for maintaining Perion's reputation for industrial excellence.

13.1. Rejection Criteria

Documents that fail to conform to these standards are subject to immediate "Withdrawal from Circulation". Specific rejection criteria include:

  • Stylistic Deviation Use of manual bolding, italics, or non-approved fonts.
  • Structural Drift Removing mandatory footer metadata or violating the H1–H2–H3 hierarchy.
  • Specification Drift Introducing performance claims in a PDS that is not supported by the TDS.
  • Accessibility Failure Exporting untagged PDFs or failing contrast requirements for critical data.

13.2. Legal and Audit Defensibility

By strictly following these governance layers, Perion ensures that its documentation is audit-safe. Every declaration — from manufacturing quality (ISO 9001) to environmental protection (RoHS/REACH) — is backed by a structured evidence layer in the Level 4 documentation stack.

Audit Defensibility — Level 5 Mandate

Every declaration in the documentation stack — from manufacturing quality (ISO 9001) to environmental protection (RoHS/REACH) — is backed by a structured evidence layer in Level 4. Deviations at Level 5 (design and brand) propagate upward to compromise the legal defensibility of Level 4 compliance declarations. This is why adherence to this manual is non-negotiable for all Perion personnel, distributors, and partners.
