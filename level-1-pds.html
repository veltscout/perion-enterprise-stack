Section 1.1

Executive Summary

The Perion Linear Perimeter Illumination Infrastructure supplies continuous, high-uniformity illumination directly along fence lines and perimeter boundaries in industrial, agricultural, and utility environments. Traditional point-source floodlights produce high-contrast conditions with intense hot spots and deep dark zones. The Perion linear, distributed low-voltage architecture eliminates these conditions by delivering light at the fence line itself.

This infrastructure achieves an 80 % reduction in power consumption relative to conventional systems while eliminating the requirement for deep civil trenching, heavy conduit, and associated high-voltage installation labour. The result is a lower total cost of ownership and faster deployment timelines. Consistent glare-controlled illumination at ≥ 10 Lux along the entire boundary optimises CCTV sensor performance and video analytics platforms by maintaining stable signal-to-noise ratios across the field of view. The IP69K rating provides the highest level of ingress protection against dust, high-pressure washdowns, and extreme environmental exposure.

The 24 V DC Safety Extra-Low Voltage (SELV) architecture simplifies compliance with health and safety requirements. The integrated Perimeter-Lock cable-continuity sensor monitors the illumination cable in real time and delivers immediate dry-contact or digital signals to the site monitoring platform in the event of any cable interruption, thereby combining illumination with perimeter monitoring in a single infrastructure layer.

Section 2

Industry Applications

The Perion Linear Perimeter Illumination Infrastructure is engineered for deployment across multiple sectors where uniform boundary illumination and simplified installation deliver measurable operational and cost advantages. In industrial manufacturing plants the system supplies high-uniformity illumination integrated with existing CCTV networks to support continuous perimeter visibility without the glare and hot spots associated with traditional floodlights. In logistics and warehouse facilities the linear distribution illuminates narrow corridors and long boundary lines between structures and fences while the 80 % power reduction contributes to lower site energy budgets.

Agricultural fencing applications benefit from the rodent-repellent cable jacket and the availability of solar-hybrid Power Packs that enable fully off-grid operation on remote boundaries. Residential estate walls utilise low-profile mounting hardware and neutral colour temperature for discreet integration that maintains the aesthetic of masonry or perimeter walls. Remote utility sites, including substations and telecommunications towers, employ the solar-hybrid configuration together with the Perimeter-Lock cable-continuity sensor to provide autonomous illumination and immediate remote alerts on any cable integrity event. Each configuration follows the same modular architecture, ensuring consistent performance and procurement simplicity regardless of site type.

Section 4

System Architecture & Components

The Perion Linear Perimeter Illumination Infrastructure utilises a linear, distributed architecture that scales by length rather than area. Power and illumination are delivered uniformly along the physical geometry of the fence or wall.

4.1.1 Power & Control Units (The Power Pack)

The Power Pack serves as the central regulation unit, converting standard AC grid power or DC solar input into stabilised 24 V DC output. It manages load over long cable runs, compensating for voltage drop so that the final LED module in a 1,000 m run receives the same regulated power as the first module. The Power Pack incorporates the interface for the Perimeter-Lock cable-continuity sensor. This interface monitors cable electrical integrity in real time. If the cable is severed, the Power Pack transmits a dry-contact relay or digital signal to the site's primary monitoring platform.

4.1.2 Illumination Cable (The Infrastructure Backbone)

The proprietary illumination cable forms the mechanical and electrical backbone. It is attached directly to the fence fabric or perimeter wall with specialised mounting accessories. The cable jacket is constructed from a UV-stabilised, rodent-repellent polymer engineered for long-term outdoor exposure in agricultural and remote utility environments.

4.1.3 Quick Connect™ LED Modules

LED modules utilise Quick Connect technology that allows crimping onto the illumination cable at any point without pre-set junction points or insulation stripping. The mechanical interface produces a gas-tight, waterproof connection rated to IP69K. Modules are spaced at 4 m intervals for standard applications or 2 m intervals for higher illuminance requirements.

4.1.4 Mounting & Ancillary Components

Specialised mounting clips and brackets are provided for Electric, ClearVu, Betafence, and masonry wall types. These components maintain cable tension and protect against mechanical stress.

Section 7

Installation & Commissioning

Installation follows a defined procedural sequence that minimises civil works.

7.1.1 Pre-Installation Checklist

Site assessment confirms fence structural integrity, Power Pack location with grid or solar access, and integration points for the Perimeter-Lock cable-continuity sensor signal.

7.1.2 Installation Sequence

  1. The illumination cable is unrolled and secured at 2.5 m – 3.0 m height using Perion mounting clips.
  2. Quick Connect LED modules are attached at the specified intervals with the crimping tool.
  3. The cable is terminated at the Power Pack and the system is energised.
  4. The Perimeter-Lock output is wired to the site monitoring platform.

7.1.3 Commissioning and Troubleshooting

Commissioning includes visual verification of spacing and functional testing of the Perimeter-Lock cable-continuity sensor. Diagnostic LEDs on the Power Pack indicate input power and cable integrity status. Individual modules can be replaced without circuit interruption.

Section 8

Maintenance, Service & Spares

The infrastructure requires minimal routine maintenance. Quarterly visual inspections confirm cable tension and absence of vegetation obstruction. In high-debris environments the IP69K rating permits periodic high-pressure washdowns. Semi-annual testing of the Perimeter-Lock cable-continuity sensor verifies correct signalling to the control room.

Recommended spares include replacement LED modules, illumination cable sections, and Power Pack units for sites subject to lightning or grid instability.

Section 9

System Design Guidelines & Example Projects

Design follows spacing rules based on site risk profile: 2.0 m spacing for critical assets (approximately 20–25 Lux), 4.0 m spacing for standard industrial sites (approximately 10–12 Lux), and 6.0 m spacing for low-risk boundaries (approximately 6–8 Lux).
