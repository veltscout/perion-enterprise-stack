Entering the high-security industrial sector requires transitioning from marketing-led documentation to a strict, enterprise-grade governance framework. This transition is codified within the Level 6 Enterprise Documentation Control Framework, which serves as the authoritative layer governing the creation, validation, storage, and distribution of every technical and commercial artifact for the Perion Linear Perimeter Illumination Infrastructure. The fundamental objective of this framework is the preservation of Technical Truth, ensuring that specifications remain consistent across the entire documentation stack and preventing the phenomenon of document drift. Document drift occurs when the integrity of technical specifications is compromised during distillation for marketing purposes or regional expansions, often leading to technical leakage or organizational liability.

The visual execution rules presented herein are aligned with Level 5 (Brand & Design) and Level 6 (Governance) mandates, designed to produce documentation that is tender-safe, industrial-grade, and print-ready. By establishing a canonical visual hierarchy, the organization ensures that technical evaluators, procurement boards, and engineers receive information in a predictable, authoritative format that signals institutional stability. This framework provides the specific parameters for cover page architecture, internal document structure, image treatment, diagrammatic standards, and data presentation, ensuring absolute consistency between Product Data Sheets (PDS) and Technical Data Sheets (TDS) while protecting sensitive engineering data from improper exposure.

1. Enterprise Documentation Governance and Hierarchy

The architecture of the documentation system is structured as a six-level vertical hierarchy, where each level maintains a distinct functional identity and target audience. This separation logic is a mandatory evolution for organizations operating in complex procurement and high-security environments, where technical engineering data must never be conflated with commercial narratives or branding guidelines.

1.1 The Six-Level Documentation Hierarchy

The hierarchical architecture ensures that every document is anchored to its functional purpose, facilitating a unidirectional flow of content validation while preventing the unauthorized crossing of data levels.

Level Functional Identity Governing Mandate Primary Audience
Level 1 Customer-Facing PDS Value proposition and market positioning. Customers, Distributors
Level 2 Technical TDS Single source of technical and engineering truth. Engineers, Technical Buyers
Level 3 Procurement & BOM Commercial execution and ordering logic. Procurement, Operations
Level 4 Compliance & Certs Evidence layer and regulatory declarations. Auditors, Tender Boards
Level 5 Brand & Design Visual system and layout control. Designers, Marketing
Level 6 Governance Authority Meta-control and structural authority. Executive Board, Auditors

Content flow originates at the Level 2 technical authority layer. Any modification to engineering specifications at this level triggers a mandatory assessment of the downstream levels, particularly Level 3 (Procurement) and Level 4 (Compliance). This ensures that the physical hardware listed in the Bill of Materials remains in absolute alignment with engineering certifications and customer-facing materials, providing a legally robust documentation ecosystem for the Perion Linear Perimeter Illumination Infrastructure.

1.2 The RACI Matrix and Decision Authority

Decision authority within the documentation lifecycle is formalized through a RACI matrix (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed), which defines the roles of Product Management, Engineering, Marketing, Operations, and the Executive Board.

Task / Activity Product Management Engineering Marketing Operations Executive
Content Creation A C C I I
Technical Validation C A I C I
Compliance Approval I R I C A
Final Release Auth C C C A C
Structural Revision C C C R A

In this framework, accountability is restricted to a single role per task to ensure clarity of ownership. Final decision authority for all technical specifications rests with the Engineering lead, while final release authorization for tender-grade documents rests with Operations. In the event of irreconcilable conflict, the Executive layer serves as the ultimate arbiter, specifically for documents carrying legal or regulatory liability.

1.3 Prevention of Technical Leakage and Document Drift

Technical leakage is defined as the improper exposure of sensitive engineering data or the compromise of technical integrity during the simplification of information for commercial use. Within the infrastructure domain, such leakage can lead to resource bleed — the loss of value due to inefficiency or failing infrastructure. The Level 6 governance framework strictly prohibits the embedding of raw laboratory test data from Level 4 into Level 1 customer-facing sheets.

Instead, Level 1 documents must utilize reference-only logic, acknowledging the existence of certifications without exposing the detailed engineering thresholds that define the system's performance. This technical-first validation ensures that marketing narratives may simplify information for readability but must never contradict the engineering truths defined in Level 2.

3. Cover Page Visual Layout Architecture

The cover page is designed to be tender-safe and print-ready, emphasizing authority and professional identity while removing all technical leakage. It must avoid specific measurements, annotations, or installation cues that could invite premature technical scrutiny.

3.1 Cover Page Zone Definition

The layout is divided into four distinct zones, each with a specific mandate and governance rule.

Zone Proportion Purpose Content Constraints
Header Zone Top 25 – 30 % Product ID & Authority Title must be the most visually dominant element. No imagery behind text.
Hero Zone Middle 35 – 40 % Visual Grounding No annotations, no measurements, no wiring visible. Stylized images only.
Callouts Band Mid-Low Strip Evaluator Scanning 5–6 canonical highlights. No qualifiers, no footnotes.
Footer Zone Bottom 10 – 15 % Governance & Metadata Confidentiality statement and document ID metadata.

3.2 Visual Execution Rules for the Cover

The visual execution must adhere to industrial-grade standards, meaning the aesthetic is institutional and calm. Secondary inset imagery is eliminated to maintain a singular, focused visual message. The background of the Header Zone must be white or very light to ensure high contrast and legibility, adhering to WCAG standards.

The Hero Visual Zone should feature a stylized perimeter fence image with linear illumination or an abstract linear light motif. This visual grounding must be achieved without technical commitment — meaning no close-ups of mounting hardware or visible power connections that could lead to technical bleed.

3.3 Key Callout Strip Protocol

The Callouts Band is designed to address the primary evaluative criteria of a tender board. These callouts are limited to a single line each, utilizing outline-style icons only. The canonical callout set is:

◈ Continuous linear illumination (≥ 10 Lux)
◈ 24 V DC SELV architecture
◈ Integrated Perimeter-Lock cable-continuity sensor
◈ Solar & grid compatible
◈ IP69K rated
◈ 5-year warranty

4. Internal Document Visual Hierarchy

Internal document structure follows a predictable logic to ensure that technical buyers can efficiently navigate from high-level summaries to dense engineering data.

4.1 Executive Summary Hierarchy

The Executive Summary is positioned as Section 1.0 and utilizes the H1_Section style (Perion Midnight Blue, Bold, Numbered). This section must provide an infrastructure-first overview, framing the system's economic advantage through reduced total cost of ownership (TCO) and civil works. It serves as a performance-driven narrative that precedes the technical specifications.

4.2 Industry Applications Section (Section 2.0)

This section bridges high-level intent with specific engineering requirements. It utilizes a multi-column structure to list core verticals such as industrial manufacturing, logistics facilities, agricultural perimeters, and remote utility sites.

Rules for Industry Application visuals:

  • PDS (Level 1): Icons may be used for vertical identification.
  • TDS (Level 2): Decorative icons are strictly prohibited. The section must include Application Schematics per Vertical — vector-based technical proof visuals for typical industrial layouts.
  • Engineering Considerations: Every vertical must include a block addressing mounting height impact, environmental exposure, and voltage drop.

4.3 Technical Table and Architecture Diagram Hierarchy

The technical core of the document (Level 2) requires the highest density of information and the most rigid styling discipline. Architecture diagrams and Lux contour maps must be provided in vector format (EPS or SVG) to ensure line-work remains sharp at all zoom levels. Technical tables must span the full width of the text column and use row banding for any table exceeding five rows to assist with tracking.

4.4 Validation and Compliance Section

Section 6.0 of the document stack is dedicated to the Evidence Layer — compliance and certifications. This section uses Body_Text_Compact for dense regulatory declarations. It must remain separate from the PDS narrative to prevent technical leakage into marketing-focused zones. If a product update moves faster than third-party lab certification, this section must remain in REVIEW or DRAFT status.

5. Standardized Image Treatment and Photography Guide

To maintain engineering credibility, all photography used in Perion documentation must adhere to industrial, realistic standards. The primary goal is to communicate test scope, provenance, and manufacturing rigor rather than aesthetic appeal.

5.1 Desaturation and Color Neutrality Rules

Documentation must be resistant to interpretation, meaning colors should be realistic and neutral to protect the record's credibility. Over-saturated colors are viewed as distortions that might hide material wear, leaks, or rust.

Photography treatment protocols:

  • Rule of Neutrality: Realistic color rendering is required. If a component is grey-blue, it must appear grey-blue, with no saturation enhancement.
  • Desaturation for Focus: In assembly or installation guides, the background may be partially desaturated (~20 – 30 %) while the target component remains in full color to create a natural call-out effect without artificial markers.
  • Black & White Standards: For long-term preservation and archival technical records, high-tonal-detail Black and White photography is acceptable as it captures texture more reliably than color.

5.2 Contrast and Lighting Protocols

The objective of industrial lighting is to maximize information density in both shadows and highlights.

Feature Technical Standard Governance Reason
Lighting Style Flat Lighting (Diffuse) Little to no shadow ensures all parts are visible.
Contrast Low to Moderate (Global) High contrast hides details in deep blacks or blown-out whites.
Local Contrast High Clarity Adjustments make textures like corrosion visible.
Depth of Field High (Deep Focus) Bokeh or blurred backgrounds are prohibited; context is vital.
Exposure Expose to the Right (ETTR) Ensures maximum shadow detail without clipping.

5.3 Prohibited Visual Elements

To maintain the infrastructure-grade standard, certain visual cues common in marketing are strictly prohibited in technical documentation:

  • No Visible Wiring: Unless the document is an installation manual, wiring must be concealed to maintain the Infrastructure Backbone aesthetic.
  • No Dramatic Lighting: Avoid God rays, extreme lens flares, or highly stylized nighttime shots that obscure the uniform light path.
  • No Inset Close-ups: Secondary imagery on the cover is removed to prevent technical leakage.

6. Diagrammatic and Iconographic Specifications

Technical diagrams are the Technical Truth layer of the documentation. They must follow international drawing standards (ISO 128) to remain legible and professional for engineering evaluators.

6.1 Diagram Line Weight and Hierarchy (ISO 128)

Line weights must be used to create visual hierarchy within schematics, preventing the clutter common in unmanaged documentation.

  • 0.25 mm — Secondary details, hatching, and dimension lines.
  • 0.50 mm — Primary components and object outlines.
  • 0.70 mm — Section boundaries and major structural separators.
Schematic Callout Requirement

All schematics must include callout boxes for ratings such as IP69K, detailing pressure, temperature, nozzle distance, and duration. Captions must be prefixed with "Caption:" and positioned directly below the visual element.

6.2 Icon Style and Selection Criteria

Iconography is governed by ISO 7000/IEC 60417 and ISO 7010. The choice between outline and filled icons is a functional, not aesthetic, decision.

Icon Context Style Rule Reason
Instructional / Functional Outline (Linear) ISO 7000 standard. Better clarity when scaled down or printed at high density.
Safety / Alert Filled (Solid) ISO 7010 standard. Solid silhouettes are detected faster in high-stress/low-light environments.
Consistency Hardware-Matched Icons must match the physical machine buttons to maintain user experience.
Custom Icon Rule — 2 px Minimum Threshold

For custom icons, the 2 px Rule applies: if the icon is smaller than 10 mm, a filled or bold outline is required to prevent visual blobbing.

7. Table Styling and Data Representation Rules

Tables are the primary mechanism for presenting complex metrics. They must be standardized to ensure that auditors can rapidly verify performance claims without wading through inconsistent formatting.

7.1 Structural Table Rules

All tables must be full-width, spanning the entire text column. Centered header alignment is preferred, while cell content must be left-aligned for rapid scanning.

Style Element Specification
Header Background Perion Midnight Blue (HEX: #0A1D33)
Header Text White, Table_Header style
Row Banding Perion Light Grey (HEX: #F5F5F7) for tables > 5 rows
Data Density TDS uses exact values; PDS uses rounded precision for evaluation.

7.2 Technical Value Formatting

The application of the Inline_Technical character style is mandatory for every electrical, optical, or environmental variable. This ensures that values such as 24 V DC, ≥ 10 Lux, or IP69K are visually distinct and audit-ready. Manual bolding of these values is prohibited; formatting must be handled by the character style map to prevent softening of data.

8. Paragraph and Character Style Maps

The Style Map is the governance mechanism for structural flow and legibility. It ensures that documents are consistent regardless of the regional office or department producing them.

8.1 Paragraph Style Map (Structural Authority)

Style Name Governing Rule Formatting Attributes
PDS_TDS_Title Mandatory for cover pages and section divider titles. Large scale, heavyweight, Midnight Blue.
PDS_TDS_Subtitle Document identifiers and straplines. Moderate scale, medium weight, Graphite.
H1_Section Major numbered headings. Bold, Midnight Blue, start on new baseline.
Body_Text Standard narrative. Restricted line length for cognitive ease.
Body_Text_Compact Legal and technical appendix. High density, reduced leading.
Callout_Text Engineering highlights. Safety Amber highlights permitted.

8.2 Character Style Map (Inline Discipline)

  • Bold_Emphasis Reserved for the first reference to key terms only.
  • Inline_Technical Mandatory for all technical metrics (Volts, Lux, IP Ratings).
  • Highlight_Value Reserved for performance differentiators that require immediate attention.
  • Italic_Reference Applied to ISO standards and regulatory references.
  • Warning_Inline Strictly for safety-critical terms within narratives.

9. Visual System Guide (One-Page Execution Specification)

This section serves as the definitive Visual System Guide for hand-off to designers or InDesign operators.

9.1 Layout & Formatting

  • Size: A4 / US Letter.
  • Margins: 20 mm (all sides).
  • Typography: Minimum 10 pt Body Text; 8 pt Technical Compact.
  • Leading: Minimum 1.2 pt for narrative text.
  • Column Structure: PDS (Standard); TDS (Dense Grid).

9.2 Color Palette (The 3-Color Rule)

No single page may exceed three primary colors (Midnight Blue, Graphite, and Safety Amber). Safety Amber is explicitly forbidden for decorative use; its presence must always indicate a safety-critical warning, a technical highlight, or an engineering note.

Color Name HEX Code Functional Role
Midnight Blue #0A1D33 Primary Authority: H1 headers, table headers, document titles.
Graphite Text #2B2E34 Primary Legibility: Body text, subheadings, technical paragraphs.
Safety Amber #F2A900 Meaning-Driven Emphasis: Warnings, highlights, light paths.
Light Grey #F5F5F7 Structure: Table banding, background blocks, pullout panels.
Chalk White #FFFFFF Support: Page background and margin zones.

9.3 Cover Page Architecture

  • No Technical Leakage: Remove specific technical specifications or wiring from the cover.
  • No Inset Imagery: Single, high-impact hero image only.
  • Subtitle: Align with Perimeter Infrastructure (Institutional tone).
  • Metadata Block: Mandatory Title, Rev, Date, Status, and Confidentiality.

9.4 Image & Diagram Treatment

  • Photography: Industrial realism. Realistic color. Flat lighting. No bokeh.
  • Desaturation: Background desaturation (20 – 30 %) permitted for focus.
  • Diagrams: Vector only. Line weights: 0.25 mm (secondary), 0.50 mm (primary), 0.70 mm (boundary).
  • Icons: Outline (functional); Filled (safety).

9.5 Technical Tables

  • Width: Full text column width. No floating tables.
  • Headers: Midnight Blue background / White text.
  • Alignment: Left-aligned data; Centered headers.
  • Banding: Light Grey for tables exceeding 5 rows.

10. Document Control and File Governance

The Perion Version Control Protocol is a non-negotiable layer designed to prevent the circulation of obsolete information.

10.1 File Naming Structure

Every controlled document must follow the mandatory naming formula:

PRN-[Level]-[Type]-[Version].pdf Example: PRN-L0-VDF-001-v2.0-FINAL.pdf

10.2 Revision and Status Controls

  • vX.0 Major Version — Triggered by hardware changes, structural layout shifts, or compliance updates.
  • v0.Y Minor Version — Triggered by editorial corrections or formatting clarifications.
  • FREEZE Tender Freeze — It is strictly forbidden to update a technical document during a live tender submission window.
